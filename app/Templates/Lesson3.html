An introduction to and Instruction in the Use of Lockpick Guns

What is a lockpick Gun ?

A lockpick gun, also known as a "snap gun" is a tool that is often used in order to rapidly force open a pin tumbler design lock without the use of the key. A slim steel rod is place into the lock and the the lockpick gun "fires" the rod against all of the pins in the lock at the same time. Therefore the cylinder is freed for a brief moment, allowing it to be turned using a tension wrench. The lockpicking gun is an alternitive to regular lockpicking techniques, such as raking.

The lockpick gun was originally developed in order to aid police forces. it allows officers to open locks without needing to spend time training additional lockpicking techniques. Even though lockpicking is often associated with crime, snap guns are seldom used by criminals as concealing the device is quite difficult.

How to Use a Lockpick Gun

The first step naturally is to insert the gun needle into the lock.

Next you should squeeze the trigger, but not all the way. First just squeeze it until the mechanism until the needle drops.

The third step is is to fully pull the trigger, the causes an internal hammer to be released which causes the needle to snap upward.

How to Use a Lockpick Gun

The Finer Points

There is a thumb wheel that determines how hard the gun snaps. Getting the adjustment on the wheel just right can take a little getting used to. It is advisable to start it on one of the lower settings.

Before the hammer falls, the needle of the gun should be at rest all along the bottom pins. You should have your wrench in place in similair manner to standard lockpicking. The tension, much like standard lockping should be very light. When the needle the snapped upward by the gun firing the top pins will be thrown upward at the same time, away from the pins on the bottom. For just a moment, when the pins are still in the air from being launched by the gun, the shear line between the shell and the plug is left wide open, and if the proper amount or torque is present on the wrench, the lock will open
